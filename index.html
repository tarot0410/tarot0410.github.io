About me

I am a 5th year PhD student in Biostatistics at University of Pittsburgh, co-advised by Drs. Ying Ding and Wei Chen. My dissertation research focuses on developing novel statistical methods for analyzing single-cell multi-omics data. My research has been supported by two grants, one from UPMC and the other from NIH, in which I play a role as Principal investigator and co-Principal investigator, respectively.

During my PhD study, I have been working as a Graduate Student Researcher at Dr. Wei Chen's Lab at UPMC Children's Hospital of Pittsburgh. One of my main responsibilities is to provide statistical support with analyzing data from scRNA-seq, single-cell multi-omics (e.g., CITE-seq, DOGMA-seq) as well as spatial transcriptomics in different tissue types, and collaborate with physicians and clinical researchers to seek novel biological discoveries.

In addition to doing methodological and collaborative research, I devote myself to serve and lead several statistical organizations and groups, including NISS Graduate Student Network and ASA Pittsburgh Student Chapter. I am also highly motivated to guide junior students or lab members with their research under supervision of my advisors.
